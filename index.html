Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that teaching is the most powerful form of communication. For me, building an IIT-level Computer Science cohort is not merely a responsibility but a privilege. I strive to create classrooms that are interest-driven, conceptually rigorous, and rooted in practical reasoning rather than rote learning.

I am comfortable teaching all core Computer Science subjects, with a special passion for Formal Languages and Automata Theory, Data Structures and Programming, Design and Analysis of Algorithms, and Database Management Systems. I am equally enthusiastic about advanced electives such as Machine Learning, Data Mining, Data Analytics, and Complex Networks.

My instructional approach follows a three-layer pedagogy model:

  • Concept clarity through board-centric explanations and intuition building.
  • Practice-driven learning using problem-solving sessions, coding demonstrations, and dataset-based experiments.
  • Recap-centric mentoring through structured handwritten-style notes that serve as lifelong academic resources.

Drawing from my PhD research on deep learning‚Äìdriven security analysis of Ethereum, I plan to introduce Security Analytics with Deep Learning as an applied module where students analyze real transaction graphs, detect anomalies, and visualize vulnerabilities.
